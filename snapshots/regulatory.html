Regulatory Hub

Keeping pace with a fast-moving regulatory environment

Payments regulation is changing faster than at any point in the last decade. We track material changes and advise clients on practical compliance and commercial response.

Europe

In progress

PSD3 and the Payment Services Regulation (PSR)

Political agreement reached late 2025. National transposition underway across EU member states; full application expected mid-to-late 2027. Covers stronger fraud protections, enhanced open banking rights and new payment institution provisions.

Updated June 2026 · Read full analysis →

Active

EU Instant Payments Regulation

All eurozone banks required to receive instant payments from January 2025 and send from October 2025. SEPA Instant Credit Transfer now mandatory infrastructure across the euro area.

Active from October 2025

Imminent

EU AI Act: high-risk system obligations

High-risk AI system requirements, covering fraud detection, credit scoring and customer risk assessment, apply from August 2026. Conformity assessments, technical documentation and human oversight mechanisms required.

August 2026 · Read full analysis →

In progress

Digital Euro Enabling Regulation

ECB completed preparatory phase late 2025. Enabling regulation expected to define the legal framework for issuance, distribution via intermediaries, holding limits and relationship to commercial bank deposits.

Expected 2026 · Read full analysis →

Active

ISO 20022 Migration

ISO 20022 becomes the default standard for high-value and cross-border payment messages globally. As SWIFT tightens tolerance for MT message translation, participants face escalating pressure to upgrade infrastructure.

Ongoing through 2025–2026

Active

EU AML Package: AMLA Authority

The EU AML package establishes AMLA with direct supervisory powers over high-risk financial institutions. Unified AML/CFT requirements across member states through a single European rulebook.

AMLA operational 2026–2027

United Kingdom

Active

FCA CASS 15: Payment Institution Safeguarding

In effect from 7 May 2026. Payment institutions and EMIs required to safeguard client funds under stricter rules including dedicated trust accounts, monthly reconciliation proof reports and updated disclosure requirements.

In force May 2026

Active

UK APP Fraud Reimbursement Rules

PSR rules require sending and receiving payment service providers to share liability for reimbursing APP fraud victims. Rules created significant commercial exposure for smaller institutions and triggered industry-wide reviews of fraud controls.

Active from October 2024

In progress

PSR CP26/1: Visa and Mastercard Financial Reporting

PSR consulted in May 2026 on requiring Visa and Mastercard to submit annual P&L accounts for UK card payment operations. Responses due 3 July 2026. Flows from card scheme fees and processing charges review.

Consultation closes July 2026

In progress

FCA Protection Reforms for Payment Institutions

Updated FCA safeguarding framework strengthens requirements for payment institutions and EMIs on how client funds are protected, intra-group settlements and reporting. Stronger focus on auditability and insolvency readiness.

Updated 2025–2026
